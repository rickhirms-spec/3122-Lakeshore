# Unit 29 Evidence Brief

## Purpose

This brief summarizes the strongest currently available evidence regarding Unit 29, the 2025-2026 restoration project, owner communications, workmanship concerns, Board review, and unresolved decision-making issues.

## Core Position

Unit 29 was identified by the Board as part of the urgent reserve fund work group and scheduled for the 2026 project phase. Owners have requested written clarification regarding scope, replacement vs reuse criteria, inspection, workmanship concerns, and consistent application of condominium rules.

## Confirmed Facts

### Unit 29 was included in the urgent reserve fund work group

The July 15, 2025 Board message stated that, based on 2024 beam assessments by Edison Engineering, owner polling, and Synergy review, sixteen units were identified as urgently requiring reserve fund work. Unit 29 was included in that group.

### Units 28-29 were deferred to the 2026 phase

The same Board message stated that the first 2025 phase would include Units 23-25 and Units 15-19, while remaining units, including Units 28-29, would be scheduled in due course for 2026.

### The Avangarde 2025 schedule did not include Unit 29

The attached Avangarde schedule covered:

- Units 23, 24, 25;
- Units 15, 16, 17, 18, 19.

It included lintel replacement, planter box repairs, coping stone repairs, and terrace waterproofing. It did not include Unit 29.

### Synergy inspected garages, terraces, and backyards in 2024

The August 2024 engineer visit notice stated that Synergy would inspect garages, terraces, and backyards to assess deficiencies at the property and common elements.

### Unit 29 owners sent a formal June 10, 2026 follow-up to John McKenzie

On June 10, 2026 at 8:40 AM, Rickhi Seecharan sent John McKenzie an email titled `Follow-Up on Outstanding Questions and Unresolved Items`, copying Gessica Zublena and Barbara.

The email asked for written responses regarding:

1. Unit 29 scope of work;
2. paver/patio stone replacement vs reuse criteria;
3. owner-funded paver replacement request;
4. owner modification approval process;
5. consistent enforcement across units;
6. inspection before completion;
7. workmanship concerns;
8. Board review status.

The email expressly stated that the owners were not seeking to direct contractors or interfere with the project.

## Key Workmanship / Project Concerns

Current owner concerns include:

- drainage and water pooling at the threshold;
- presence of only one drainage hole;
- gap at the threshold;
- excess sealant;
- reused or damaged pavers;
- patio screen door damage;
- damage to landscaping / rubble concerns;
- lack of clear final inspection process;
- uncertainty over who signs off on completed work.

## Evidence Supporting Timeliness Concern

The June 10, 2026 follow-up email stated that when work is underway, delayed responses can effectively become denial without a clear decision because the work proceeds and the opportunity to address the issue is lost.

This is important because the owners requested written direction before the work affecting Unit 29 was accepted as complete.

## Comparative Evidence

A forwarded email regarding Unit 28 from Barbara McAdam raised similar concerns regarding balcony repairs, inspection, sign-off, deficiencies, corrective actions, and Board receipt.

This supports the view that workmanship/inspection concerns may not be isolated to Unit 29.

## Open Questions

1. What exact deficiencies were identified for Unit 29 by Synergy, Edison, or any other consultant?
2. What was the approved scope of work for Unit 29?
3. Who approved the final Unit 29 scope?
4. What criteria determine whether pavers/stones are replaced, reused, repaired, or owner-funded?
5. Was the Board asked to approve or deny the owner-funded paver replacement request?
6. Was the request approved, denied, deferred, or never brought to the Board?
7. Who is responsible for final inspection/sign-off?
8. What documentation confirms the project was completed to an acceptable standard?
9. Are the same standards being applied to Units 28 and 29 and other similarly affected units?
10. Are there written contractor deficiencies, punch lists, change orders, or inspection reports?
