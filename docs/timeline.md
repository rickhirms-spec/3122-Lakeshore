# Timeline — HCC 109 / Unit 29

## 2024

### March 1, 2024 — MRCM appointed
Maple Ridge Community Management became property manager for HCC 109. Introduction letter assigned Christie Henderson as property manager and directed owners to contact customer service for urgent matters.

### May 10, 2024 — Garage beam repairs notice
Notice identified steel beams in garage ceilings requiring repairs. Work schedule ran May 15 to June 21, 2024. Residents were not permitted in affected garages during work and had to clear areas around beams.

### July 31, 2024 — Beam project completion
Community notice stated beam project completed, with major reinforcement on three units and preventative reinforcement on other units.

### August 13, 2024 — Synergy engineer visit
Notice said Synergy engineer would assess garages and terraces and might need garage access.

### August 19, 2024 — Synergy engineer visit expanded
Notice said Synergy engineer would assess garages, terraces, and backyards, noting deficiencies in property/common elements. Security guard would escort engineer.

## 2025

### April 24, 2025 — Financial update / reserve fund meeting
Referenced later as the meeting where reserve-fund work and funding challenges were discussed.

### May 30, 2025 — Reserve fund funding letter
Board/management advised that pending repairs would be funded through increased monthly fees and special assessments.

### June 9, 2025 — 2025 operating budget
Common fees adjusted. For Unit 29, July–December 2025 monthly fee became $643.76 due to catch-up after delayed increase.

### June 25, 2025 — 2025 special assessment
Corporation-wide special assessment of $280,000. Unit 29 total $8,708.01, paid as $2,902.67 x 3.

### July 15, 2025 — Board construction message
Board said Synergy had specified and tendered first stage reserve-fund work, awarded to Avangarde Restoration Corp. Work included terrace waterproofing and masonry/lintel repairs. Units #4, #13–25, and #28–29 identified for urgent work over 2025 and 2026.

### July/August 2025 — Avangarde schedule
Schedule identified task categories including lintel replacement, planter box repairs, stair coping stone repairs, and terrace waterproofing.

### August 21, 2025 — Construction delay
Notice stated work at units 23–25 stopped due to delay securing engineering documents required by contractor. Delay affected timing of next phase.

### October 2025 — Community newsletter
Newsletter says Unit 29 walkway re-leveling had been completed and broken walkway stones would be replaced later all at once.

### November 28, 2025 — 2026 operating budget
Common element fees increased 9.894%. Daily operating expenses held to 0% increase. Increase tied to Reserve Fund Study and pending work.

### December 2025 — Records request dispute
John McKenzie and Michelle Kelly responded to owner records/special assessment correspondence. Position given: prescribed CAO form required; assessment not suspended by email request.

### December 22, 2025 — 2026 special assessment
Corporation-wide assessment $250,000. Unit 29 total $7,775.00, paid $1,943.75 on Feb 1, May 1, Aug 1, Nov 1 2026. Letter anticipated another $250,000 assessment in 2027.

## 2026

### February 2026 — Newsletter
Newsletter repeated walkway re-leveling references including Unit 29 and stated 2026 work would be similar but concentrated on west side.

### February 15, 2026 — Unit 29 statement
Statement showed Feb 1 common fee plus special assessment returned/NSF, plus $55 returned check charge and $28.25 late fee.

### April 10, 2026 — Construction plans for 2026
Notice said remaining urgent units, including #28–29, would be repaired in 2026. Described first stage reserve work as terrace waterproofing and masonry/lintel repairs.

### May 21, 2026 — Unit 29 construction date
John McKenzie advised Unit 29 work scheduled to start May 26, 2026 and last until at least June 15. Required patio clearing and garage access/clearing. Did not provide itemized Unit 29 scope.

### June 2026 — Current dispute
Issues include paver replacement/reuse, owner-funded paver replacement request, drainage/threshold water pooling, workmanship, contractor communication allegations, Board review status, and unanswered scope questions.
